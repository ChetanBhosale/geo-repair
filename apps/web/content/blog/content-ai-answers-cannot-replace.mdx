eNo content is fully AI-proof. If content can be summarized, AI systems may summarize it. The better question is which content is hardest to replace without a click, a tool, or the original source.

The answer is content that contains unique value, not generic explanation.

## What content is hardest to replace?

The most durable formats tend to be:

- Original research and first-party data
- Calculators and interactive tools
- Templates and checklists people can use
- Deep case studies
- Benchmarks
- Strong opinions from real experience
- Community discussions and reviews
- Product documentation with precise implementation detail

AI can describe these assets, but it cannot always replace the utility or provenance behind them.

## Why do generic explainers struggle?

Generic explainers are easy to summarize. A page that answers "what is X" with the same consensus as every other page gives users little reason to click and gives AI systems little reason to cite it.

That does not mean definitions are useless. It means definitions need to be connected to something deeper: examples, tools, data, comparison, implementation, or a unique point of view.

## What should you create instead?

Ask whether the page gives the reader something they could not get from a generic answer:

- A calculation
- A decision framework
- A downloadable template
- A real example
- A product-specific workflow
- A benchmark
- A comparison table with clear criteria
- A first-hand lesson

If the answer is no, the page may be replaceable.

## How does this help AI search?

Durable content is more sourceable. AI systems need sources for original facts, tools, examples, and claims. If your page becomes the best source for something specific, it has a better chance of being cited than a page that simply restates common knowledge.

Do not try to make content impossible to summarize. Make it too useful to ignore.
