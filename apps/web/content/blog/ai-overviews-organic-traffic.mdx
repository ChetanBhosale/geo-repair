AI Overviews can reduce organic traffic for some queries, especially simple informational searches where the generated answer satisfies the user. But "AI Overviews kill traffic" is too broad to be useful.

The better question is which queries lose clicks, which pages can become sources, and which buyer intents still deserve deeper content.

## Where are clicks most at risk?

Clicks are most at risk when the query asks for a short, factual answer:

- Definitions
- Basic how-to steps
- Simple comparisons
- Quick troubleshooting
- Generic informational questions

If the answer can be summarized in a few lines, many users may not need to click.

## Where can content still win?

Content remains valuable when it provides something the answer panel cannot fully replace:

- Original data
- Tools and calculators
- Templates and checklists
- Product details
- Case studies
- Strong opinions from experience
- Detailed comparisons
- Local, pricing, or implementation context

AI can summarize those pages, but the page still carries the source value.

## How do you diagnose AI Overview impact?

Use a query-level process:

1. Find pages with declining clicks.
2. Check whether impressions stayed stable.
3. Look for CTR drops.
4. Inspect the actual search result.
5. Note whether an AI Overview appears.
6. Check whether your URL, competitor URLs, or third-party sources are cited.

Do not assume every traffic drop is caused by AI. Seasonality, rankings, snippets, ads, and demand shifts can all change traffic.

## What is the practical response?

Do not abandon informational content. Make it more defensible:

- Answer directly
- Add original examples
- Use structured data
- Improve internal links
- Create tools where a static answer is not enough
- Build pages worth citing, not just pages worth indexing

AI Overviews change the value of clicks, but they do not remove the need for trusted sources. The sites that adapt will measure both traffic and source visibility.
