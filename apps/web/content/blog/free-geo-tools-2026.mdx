## GEO Repair free AI search readiness checkup

GEO Repair is for teams that want to know whether their site is technically ready for AI search, then fix the blocker if the report points to code.

The free checkup scans a public URL and reviews the things AI crawlers can actually fetch:

- Server-rendered content in the raw HTML
- robots.txt, sitemap, and llms.txt coverage
- Titles, descriptions, canonicals, Open Graph, and Twitter metadata
- Structured data that matches the visible page
- Heading structure, internal links, and answer-first sections
- Citation quality and sourceable claims

The useful difference is the repair path. If the report finds a blocker in a Next.js, React, or custom-coded site, the paid workflow can open a reviewable pull request instead of leaving the audit as another backlog item.

Use GEO Repair when the question is: **what is blocking this site, and can we ship the fix?**

## What most free GEO tools do not do

Most free tools stop at one of these outputs:

- A score
- A crawler or schema warning
- A visibility snapshot
- A content recommendation
- A generated file such as llms.txt

Those outputs can be helpful, but they do not change your site. If the issue is a client-rendered pricing page, broken canonical, stale sitemap, missing schema, or blocked crawler rule, someone still has to make the edit.

That is where an audit-to-fix workflow matters. Use free tools to find the problem. Use a repair workflow when the problem lives in code, routing, metadata, structured data, crawl files, or content structure.

## A simple tool stack

For most teams, the practical stack is small:

1. Run a free readiness scan on your homepage and highest-intent pages.
2. Validate metadata, schema, robots.txt, sitemap, and llms.txt.
3. Review one or two money pages with a page-level content scorer.
4. Check whether AI systems mention your brand for obvious buyer prompts.
5. Turn technical blockers into tickets or pull requests.

Do that before paying for a monitoring platform. Visibility tracking is useful, but it works better after the site is crawlable, structured, and answerable.
