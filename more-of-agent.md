1/ Build brand authority first
- It's the #1 predictor of citations.
- Authority outweighs clever on-page tricks.
- No authority, no mentions. Period.

2/ Get cited off your own site
- 85% of brand mentions come from third-party pages.
- Directories, reviews, "best of" lists.
- Your domain alone won't carry you.

3/ Structure content for extraction
- Self-contained 50-150 word chunks.
- Question-based headings.
- Answer in the first 50 words.

4/ Add comparison tables
- They earn ~32% more citations.
- LLMs love clean, structured comparisons.
- "Best X for Y" queries force retrieval.

5/ Publish original data
- Surveys, case studies, real numbers.
- It makes you the source others cite.
- Generic summaries get skipped.

6/ Track share of answer, not rankings
- Measure how often you're mentioned.
- 15%+ share is strong. 25%+ is leading.
- Old SEO KPIs don't apply here.
